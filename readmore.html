Featured Books

To Kill a Mockingbird
Harper Lee

To Kill a Mockingbird

Harper Lee

1960 Classic Fiction

A powerful and compassionate drama about coming of age in a South poisoned by racism and hatred.

Set in the American South during the 1930s, the novel follows the journey of Scout Finch, her brother Jem, and their father Atticus, a lawyer who defends a Black man accused of raping a white woman. Through Scout's eyes, the reader witnesses racial prejudice, moral corruption, and social inequality in a small Alabama town.

Pages: 281
Language: English
ISBN: 978-0061120084
Awards: Pulitzer Prize for Fiction (1961)
1984
George Orwell

1984

George Orwell

1949 Dystopian Fiction

A chilling portrait of a totalitarian society under the ever-watchful eye of Big Brother.

The novel introduces the concepts of omnipresent government surveillance, historical negationism, and propaganda in service of a cult of personality around a dictatorial leader. Winston Smith, the protagonist, works at the Ministry of Truth, rewriting historical documents to match the ever-changing party line.

Pages: 328
Language: English
ISBN: 978-0451524935
Legacy: Introduced terms like "Big Brother," "doublethink," and "thoughtcrime" into popular culture
Pride and Prejudice
Jane Austen

Pride and Prejudice

Jane Austen

1813 Classic Romance

A classic tale of love, social class, and personal growth in early 19th-century England.

The novel follows the character development of Elizabeth Bennet, who learns about the repercussions of hasty judgments and comes to appreciate the difference between superficial goodness and actual goodness. Mr. Darcy experiences a similar journey, as he sheds his prideful demeanor to win Elizabeth's heart.

Pages: 279
Language: English
ISBN: 978-0141439518
Fun Fact: One of the most popular novels in English literature with over 20 million copies sold
The Great Gatsby
F. Scott Fitzgerald

The Great Gatsby

F. Scott Fitzgerald

1925 Classic American Literature

A vibrant portrait of the Jazz Age and the American Dream's corruption through wealth and excess.

Set in the summer of 1922, the novel explores themes of decadence, idealism, social upheaval, resistance to change, and excess. It creates a portrait of the Roaring Twenties that has been described as a cautionary tale regarding the American Dream.

Pages: 180
Language: English
ISBN: 978-0743273565
Impact: Initially a commercial disappointment, now considered a literary classic
One Hundred Years of Solitude
Gabriel García Márquez

One Hundred Years of Solitude

Gabriel García Márquez

1967 Magical Realism

A landmark novel that tells the multi-generational story of the Buendía family in the fictional town of Macondo.

The magical realist style and thematic focus on the inevitable repetition of history help the novel chronicle seven generations of the Buendía family in the fictional town of Macondo, Colombia. The novel explores the genre of magical realism by emphasizing the extraordinary nature of commonplace things while magical elements are presented as mundane.

Pages: 417
Language: Spanish (originally)
ISBN: 978-0060883287
Awards: Nobel Prize in Literature (1982, author)
The Alchemist
Paulo Coelho

The Alchemist

Paulo Coelho

1988 Philosophical Fiction

An inspiring fable about following your dreams and listening to your heart.

The story follows a young Andalusian shepherd named Santiago on his journey to Egypt, after having a recurring dream of finding treasure there. The book is an allegory about the importance of pursuing one's dreams and destiny, referred to in the novel as one's "Personal Legend".

Pages: 197
Language: Portuguese (originally)
ISBN: 978-0061122415
Sales: Over 150 million copies worldwide
